# chase.md — Demo Script for Stepdad

_5-minute walkthrough. Conversational tone — you're showing a product, not pitching a VC._

---

## 1. The Problem (60 seconds)

> "So you know how every year around January your team starts chasing clients for their records — P60s, bank statements, receipts, all that stuff?
>
> I looked into it. The average UK practice spends **500 to 750 hours a year** just chasing documents. That's emails, phone calls, follow-ups, chasing the same people three or four times. And it's the worst kind of work — nobody trained as an accountant to send reminder emails.
>
> Most practices still do this manually. Maybe a spreadsheet to track who's sent what. Some use Senta or Karbon but the chasing part is still pretty manual.
>
> That's what chase.md fixes."

## 2. What It Does (60 seconds)

> "It's a document chasing tool built specifically for accountancy practices. Three key things:
>
> **Multi-channel chasing** — it sends chasers via email, SMS, and WhatsApp. Not just one channel. If someone ignores the email, it escalates to a text. Then a WhatsApp. You set the schedule and it just runs.
>
> **AI document recognition** — when a client uploads something, the system automatically classifies it. It knows the difference between a P60 and a bank statement. So your team doesn't have to manually check and file everything.
>
> **Client portal** — each client gets a simple upload link. No login, no password, no app to install. They click the link, see what's outstanding, drag and drop their files. Done."

## 3. The Demo (3 minutes)

### Dashboard (30s)

> "This is your practice dashboard. You can see at a glance: how many documents are outstanding, which clients have responded, which are overdue. Think of it like a heatmap of who needs chasing."

_Show the dashboard. Point out the key metrics._

### Create a Campaign (45s)

> "Let's say it's January and you need P11Ds and bank statements from all your personal tax clients. You create a campaign."

_Click 'New Campaign'. Walk through:_
- Name it ("2025/26 Personal Tax")
- Select which document types you need
- Import clients (show CSV import or Xero sync)
- Set the chase schedule (e.g., email day 1, SMS day 5, WhatsApp day 10)

> "Once you launch it, chase.md handles everything. Your team doesn't touch it unless someone replies with a question."

### Client Experience (45s)

> "Now let me show you what the client sees."

_Open the client portal link (the `/p/[token]` route) in a new tab or on your phone._

> "They get a message — email, text, whatever — with a link. They tap it, they see a simple page: 'Hi John, Smith & Co needs these documents from you.' They see a checklist of what's outstanding. They drag and drop their files. That's it.
>
> No login. No app. Works on their phone. Takes 30 seconds."

### Document Arrives (45s)

> "Back on the practice side — the document comes in and the AI classifies it. It says 'this looks like a P60 for tax year 2024/25' and matches it to the right client and the right request.
>
> Your team gets a notification. The dashboard updates. The client stops getting chased for that document.
>
> If they uploaded the wrong thing — say a utility bill instead of a bank statement — it flags it and the chase continues."

## 4. Close (30 seconds)

> "So that's it. You set up a campaign, the system chases, clients upload through a dead-simple portal, AI sorts the documents, and your team only gets involved when something needs a human.
>
> We're building it for UK practices specifically — Xero integration, GDPR compliant, priced per practice not per user. I'd love to get your feedback on whether this matches how your practice actually works."

---

## Tips

- **Show it on your phone** for the client portal bit — makes it tangible
- **Ask questions** — "Is that how your team does it currently?" gets him engaged
- **Don't oversell the AI** — say "it classifies documents" not "it uses advanced machine learning"
- **Mention Xero** — if he uses it, the sync feature will click immediately
- If he asks about price: "We're looking at £49-149/month depending on practice size, still finalising"
